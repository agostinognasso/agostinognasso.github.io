About Me

I am a Doctor of Economics from the University of Naples Federico II, specialising in the intersection of statistical methodology and machine learning interpretability. My research focuses on Explainable Machine Learning (XML) — building frameworks that make powerful black-box algorithms transparent, interpretable, and actionable for decision-makers.

My core contribution is the development of E2Tree (Explainable Ensemble Trees), a methodology that represents Random Forest and other ensemble models through interpretable decision tree structures, preserving predictive accuracy while delivering faithful explanations. This work spans both classification and regression contexts and has been published in leading statistical journals.

Beyond interpretability, I apply quantitative methods to health economics and social analytics — from predicting depression risk in survey data to evaluating hospital service quality. I am equally committed to open science: as a member of the K-Synth Research Lab and the Bibliometrix development team, I contribute to tools used by thousands of researchers worldwide for systematic literature reviews and science mapping.

Research Areas

Explainable AI (XAI)

Making black-box models transparent through interpretable tree structures and visual explanations. Development and extension of E2Tree — a global surrogate framework for ensemble models that delivers faithful, human-readable explanations across classification and regression contexts.

Machine Learning & Ensembles

Random Forests, XGBoost, and ensemble methods with a focus on interpretability. Investigation of divergence measures and stochastic frameworks for evaluating model reconstruction quality — formalising the notion of interpretability quality itself.

Bibliometrics & Science Mapping

Quantitative analysis of scientific literature, citation networks, and research trends. Contributing to open-source tools (bibliometrix, Biblioshiny) used globally by researchers for systematic literature reviews and science mapping analysis.

Applied Health Analytics

Application of ML interpretability to clinical and health survey data: depression prediction, hospital service quality evaluation, and evidence-based decision support. Research within the PRIN-funded SciK-Health and AHS Centres projects.

Applied Economics

Statistical and machine learning methods applied to economic phenomena: composite indicators, stochastic evaluation frameworks, and policy-relevant analysis of public sector performance. Bridge between advanced methodology and applied economic research.

Applied Quantitative Methods

Cross-domain application of quantitative methods to heterogeneous data — from social surveys and bibliographic corpora to environmental and geophysical datasets. Flexible methodological approach spanning economics, social science, and natural science contexts.

Projects & Software

e2tree
CRAN v0.2.0
Explainable Ensemble Trees

The e2tree package implements the Explainable Ensemble Trees (E2Tree) methodology — a global surrogate model designed to approximate the prediction mechanism of tree-based ensembles (Random Forests, XGBoost, Boosting) through a single, interpretable tree structure. Unlike CART trees that optimise a loss function directly on data, E2Tree learns the relational structure established by the black-box model by extracting a co-occurrence matrix from the trained ensemble and applying hierarchical clustering to construct a transparent dendrogram.

Key Features

  • Global Interpretability — converts complex ensemble models into a single explanatory tree
  • Dual Mode — supports Classification (frequency-based connectivity) and Regression (weighted connectivity)
  • Visual Insights — dendrogram-like visualisations and clear decision rules for each cluster
  • XGBoost Support — extended to gradient boosting in v0.2.0
  • rpart Integration — outputs compatible with rpart objects for seamless downstream analysis

bibliometrix
CRAN v5.2.1
Comprehensive Science Mapping Analysis

An R package for quantitative research in scientometrics and bibliometrics. Provides a comprehensive workflow for science mapping analysis, supporting data import from major bibliographic databases including Scopus, Web of Science, Dimensions, OpenAlex, PubMed, Cochrane Library, and Lens. As a core developer team member, I contribute to development, maintenance, and the web interface Biblioshiny.

Key Features

  • Import and convert data from 7+ bibliographic databases
  • Descriptive bibliometric analysis — author productivity, citation analysis, h-index
  • Network matrices for co-citation, coupling, collaboration, and co-word analysis
  • Thematic maps, thematic evolution plots, and conceptual structure visualisations
  • Interactive web interface via Biblioshiny for non-coders
Aria, M. & Cuccurullo, C. (2017). bibliometrix: An R-tool for comprehensive science mapping analysis. Journal of Informetrics, 11(4), 959–975.
